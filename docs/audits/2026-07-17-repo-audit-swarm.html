01

Executive summary

Four read-only lanes covered the repository from complementary angles (seam/architecture drift, integrity/guardrail gaps, blast-radius/choke-point sizing, and test-verification readiness) and converged cleanly: one genuine overlap (the unwired Vitest CI step) merged from two lanes, and otherwise 19 distinct findings survived with no other duplication. The most load-bearing find is the merged CI gap — a real test suite exists but nothing runs it, a one-line fix with a known blocker (workflow-scope token) already tracked. The most serious data-integrity find is athlete_states' NOT NULL/nullable drift with two demonstrated crash paths, a direct re-scope of the already-tracked AUD-C12 item. One candidate — the state_bridge.py choke point where INT-05 and INT-15 converge — carries human_decision_risk 4 and is held as blocked-needs-human-decision pending a sequencing call. The rest is a mix of quick documentation/dependency fixes, a couple of coordinated multi-file sweeps, and lower-urgency architecture-fitness observations.

This repo has an active, well-documented integrity workstream (INT-NN / AUD series) with prior audit rounds through 2026-07-16. Lanes were briefed on that history and instructed to verify current status rather than re-litigate known items; four candidates here are explicitly re-scoped known-open items (marked KNOWN, RE-SCOPED below), not fresh discoveries.

The four lane health scores (6, 6, 7, 7 of 10) average to 6.5 — solid, above-median engineering discipline, not exemplary. Corroborated by severity distribution: of 20 candidates only one hits severity 4, none hit 5, median severity is 2. The backend has unusually mature verification infrastructure (real-DB test harness, migration round-trip gate, drift gate, boot guards) that keeps most findings contained and quickly fixable (13 of 20 are direct-fix with locality≥4). What holds this back from a B/B+: one candidate requiring an explicit architecture-sequencing decision (state_bridge.py), a genuine security-adjacent bug in production auth code (email normalization), and a frontend test seam that is real in name only (1 of 77 files covered, and its one CI-enforceable gate has sat unwired since the day it was created).

        Verification readiness

        The Python side can prove almost any backend fix quickly and honestly: real per-worker Postgres fixtures built from actual Alembic migrations, a migration up/down/up round-trip gate, a meta-test that catches assertion-less tests, and parallel ruff+pyright+coverage gates in CI with no skip/xfail abuse. Testability scores of 4–5 for backend-scoped candidates (user-repo-email-normalization-bug, athlete-states-notnull-drift, web-ci-missing-vitest-step, readme-stale-test-count) are well-founded. Two categories carry an asterisk: anything scoped to web/ can only currently be proven by a human running pnpm test locally, since CI enforces nothing there yet; and anything touching app/ml, app/scripts, or app/analysis rests on ad hoc functional tests with no enforced floor, since both are explicitly excluded from the pyright and coverage gates. Any queue execution landing a frontend fix should treat "tests exist and pass locally" as necessary but not sufficient until web-ci-missing-vitest-step itself is resolved.
